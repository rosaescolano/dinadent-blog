Today's fast-paced lifestyle and prolonged stress situations have a direct reflection in our mouths. One of the most diagnosed disorders in recent years is bruxism, the involuntary habit of clenching or grinding teeth, which occurs mainly during deep sleep hours, although it can also manifest during the day.

Since it occurs unconsciously, most patients do not discover they suffer from bruxism until physical consequences appear. The most common symptoms are waking up with pain or fatigue in the jaw muscles, morning headaches located in the temples, neck pain, and cervical tension. At a dental level, the excessive force causes accelerated enamel wear, micro-fractures in the teeth, loss of tooth height, and even damage to existing prosthetic treatments or implants. Furthermore, it can severely damage the temporomandibular joint (TMJ), causing clicking sounds or locking when opening the mouth.

The primary treatment to stop bruxism damage is designing a rigid stabilization splint or nightguard. This resin device is custom-made for the patient's mouth after taking digital impressions. It is worn during the night and acts as a shock absorber: it prevents teeth from making direct contact, dissipates bite forces, and allows facial muscles and the joint to rest in an ideal anatomical position.
